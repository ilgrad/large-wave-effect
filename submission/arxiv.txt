ABSTRACT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
We study the large-wave effect for free oscillations of the periodic discrete
chain (the ring of $N$ beads) and for the discrete Schrodinger equation on the
same ring. For the unit-impulse Green's function $A_N=\sup_t\max_j|u_j(t)|$,
Bohr's theorem identifies the supremum with the $\ell^1$ mass of the Fourier
coefficients when the frequencies $\{2\sin(\pi r/N)\}$ are rationally independent.
Our main result is that the order is settled for every $N$: $A_N\sim(1/\pi)\ln N$,
with $A_N=(1/\pi)\ln N+O(\ln\ln\ln N)$. The lower bound rests on a palindromic
argument showing the first $\varphi(2N)/2$ frequencies are $\mathbb{Q}$-independent,
so a $(1-o(1))$ fraction of the ceiling $U_N=(1/2N)\sum\csc(\pi r/N)$ can always be
aligned. We further prove that $A_N=U_N$ if and only if $N$ is prime or a power of
two -- equivalently, every integer frequency relation has coefficient-sum divisible
by four -- via an explicit root-of-unity obstruction; the orbit-closure subtorus has
dimension exactly $\varphi(2N)/2$. The composite deficit $U_N-A_N$ is $O(\ln\ln\ln N)$,
and the order law extends to the Dirichlet segment and to every Cartesian product
$C_N\,\square\,H$. For the discrete Schrodinger propagator $e^{-itL_N}$, unitarity
forbids amplification of a localized state, while the $\ell^\infty\to\ell^\infty$
amplification is $B_N=\Theta(\sqrt N)$ with $\liminf B_N/\sqrt N\ge c_0/\sqrt2$ proved;
the constant splits by the parity of $N$ (numerically even $N\to c_0/\sqrt2$, odd
$N\to\beta_{odd}=0.928\ldots$, no elementary closed form found), so
$\limsup\ge\beta_{odd}$. For the focusing discrete NLS the linear large wave persists under weak
nonlinearity, the modulational-instability band is read off the $L_N$ spectrum, and
the on-site breather exists and is stable. Proved results are separated throughout
from numerical evidence; all quantitative claims are reproduced by accompanying
scripts and an exact GAP/PARI/FriCAS/Rocq layer.
